How I spend my free time

Some of the things I like to do with my free time are listed below with some links!

Click to open!

You can search in the Ottawa Reddit for things to do.

  1. Taylor Swift. I grew up listening to TS and have so much fun listening to her music. Some of it takes me back to certain times in my life and I think it’s awesome that music holds memories for me.
  2. Restaurants. Ottawa has an absolutely fantastic restaurant scene. One thing I do when I find a new restaurant to try is I always read through the entire menu. I don’t really drink anymore so it’s really nice to see menus with non-alcoholic options that are just as fun as regular cocktails.
  3. Day trips. The city is in prime location for day trips. Even overnight trips to Montreal or Toronto are an option. It’s fun planning a hike or to go someplace I’ve never been to that’s at least an hour away. I try to go somewhere new since I haven’t seen a ton of Ontario yet.
  4. Chill days. Sometimes I’ll bike to Mooney’s Bay and read my book. Friends and I will go for walks and grab an ice cream or coffee, which is a nice alternative to going out for drinks. If I’m in the middle of a good book, I’ll sit out on my balcony and relax. During the winter I tend to hibernate so I really have to force myself to get outside for a walk.

These are a few of my favourite books

Title Author Thoughts
The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo Taylor Jenkins Reid This book was extremely entertaining. There was always something happening and Reid wrote each character to be unique.
Educated Tara Westover I read Educated for the first time during the pandemic. There were some moments where I was confused as to what timeline I was in because she jumps around. Westover provided a vivid story of her life, leaving me shocked several times.
The Kite Runner Khaled Hosseini Hosseini created a beautiful, tragic story that left me speechless at 15-years-old. Since then, I read this book every few years and I always pick up on new things I missed.
